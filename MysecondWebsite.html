Change Management

Change Management Process

The process is responsible for controlling the lifecycle of all service changes. The primary objective of Change Management is to enable beneficial changes to be made, with minimum disruption to services.

Key Activities

  • Receive requests for change
  • Review change request and create Change ticket in NetSuite
  • Categorise Change for Risk
  • Perform service impact analysis
  • Record service information (service ID, responsible component SID)
  • Determine schedule taking into account other Changes and resource availability
    • Identify systems, labels, documents, drawings and records that will need to be updated
  • Notify requester if change has been approved and confirm schedule
  • Notify customers
  • Monitor Change during activity wwindow
  • Manage internal and external communications
  • Manage supplier, technical and managerial escalations
  • Ensure systems, labels, documents, drawings and records are updated after the change

The change request should contain the following information:

  1. Name of requester
  2. Reason for Change
  3. Description of the proposed change
  4. Impact (outage or advisory)
  5. List of affected components (circuits, services, equipment, facilities)
  6. Proposed start and end date / time (UTC), less than 10 business daysâ€™ notice (or 1 day for access) will be considered an Emergency Request
  7. Roll back plan
  8. Resources required from WIOCC (if any)
